## Design Context

### Users
Adults (primarily 21+, though filters make it family-adaptable) gathered in person around a board game. They've been invited by a friend — they arrive with context, not questions. Primary job: quickly get into a game. Secondary job: understand what Chance is and request access if they don't have it yet.

### Brand Personality
**Irreverent. Flirty. Neon.**

The app has a latent sense of danger — something's about to happen, and you might regret it, but you'll enjoy it. It's the energy of a dive bar that somehow looks incredible. Confident without explaining itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
**References:** Grindr (dark, direct, high-contrast, no hand-holding, slightly dangerous confidence) × Roger Rabbit (chaos energy, unexpected pops of color against noir, the feeling that the frame might break at any moment).

**Anti-references:** Trivial Pursuit. No corporate blues, no quiz-show sanitization, no committee-approved family-night palettes. Nothing beige. Nothing that says "everyone goes home happy."

**Theme:** Dark-only. Deep navy (#0e0f1a) base. Amber (#d4a847) is the neon accent — used surgically with glow effects. Purple (#8b7fe8) is structural (borders, active states). Cormorant Garamond at large, dramatic sizes for display moments. DM Sans for UI copy.

**The neon rule:** Amber glow is the single "electric" element. It should appear on: the amber rule beneath the wordmark, the "Create an account" CTA, and one or two key accent moments. Never purple glow — purple is cool and structural, not alive.

### Design Principles
1. **Don't explain yourself.** The home screen is for friends who were invited, not strangers browsing. Atmosphere > onboarding copy.
2. **One focal point per screen.** The wordmark is the hero on Home. Everything else exists to support the action area below it. Never compete with the focal point.
3. **Amber earns attention, purple sets structure.** Amber (#d4a847) with glow = alive, actionable, worth noticing. Purple (#8b7fe8) = the grid, the rules, the active state. Mix them with intention.
4. **Size contrast is your voice.** 88px wordmark next to 12px caption text. That gap is the personality. Flatten it and you get Trivial Pursuit.
5. **Left-aligned, bottom-anchored.** Centered layouts are polite and forgettable. Left-align the drama. Anchor the hero to the bottom of its flex region so it feels grounded, not floating.
